Project Objectives:
The objective of this project is to transform the existing library space into dedicated STEM classrooms by June 2025. We aim to renovate a 50x75 foot area, relocating 50% of the books and donating the remainder.  The project will create three separate classrooms equipped for Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math instruction, adhering to Energy Star guidelines. 
Deliverables:            
The deliverables for this project includes a project plan that consists of stakeholder interviews, architect selection, concept plans, concept plan meeting, design approval, furniture ordering, and general contractor selection. 
Milestones:            
The STEM Classroom Renovation project aims to transform a 50x75 foot library space into three dedicated STEM classrooms by June 2025, involving book relocation, demolition, construction, and furnishing. Key milestones include completing planning activities by June 11, 2025, starting demolition after June 6, 2025, and finalizing all construction by August 11, 2025, while adhering to Energy Star guidelines and optimizing educational grant funds. The project encompasses of three main phases: project planning, execution, and closeout, with specific work packages and deliverables for each phase. 
Technical Requirements:          
The project requires a permit since there will be alterations to the mechanical and electrical systems. Board of directors for the school requires all construction projects to meet Energy Star program guidelines. Workers who enters the campus will need to check in at the facility building to ensure they have their work badges to prevent unauthorized personnel. 
Limits and Exclusions:            
The project limits include restricting physical work inside the building until after June 6, 2025, when students are no longer present. Construction activities must be completed by August 11, 2025, to avoid disrupting the new academic year. Weekend and holiday work should be minimized to control costs. Additionally, the project excludes any alterations to the existing exterior walls bounding the renovation area.
